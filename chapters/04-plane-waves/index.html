Chapter 4 — Plane Waves

Electromagnetic waves in lossless and lossy media, polarization, and interfaces

4.1 Uniform Plane Wave Solutions

For a source-free region ($\rho = 0$, $\mathbf{J} = 0$), the wave equation gives solutions of the form:

Eq. 4.1 $$\tilde{\mathbf{E}}(z) = E_0\,e^{-\gamma z}\,\hat{\mathbf{x}}, \qquad \tilde{\mathbf{H}}(z) = \frac{E_0}{\eta}\,e^{-\gamma z}\,\hat{\mathbf{y}}$$

where $\gamma = \alpha + j\beta$ is the propagation constant and $\eta$ is the intrinsic impedance of the medium.

4.2 Lossless Media

In a lossless dielectric ($\sigma = 0$):

$$\alpha = 0, \qquad \beta = \omega\sqrt{\mu\varepsilon}, \qquad \eta = \sqrt{\frac{\mu}{\varepsilon}}$$

Phase velocity and wavelength:

$$v_p = \frac{\omega}{\beta} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu\varepsilon}}, \qquad \lambda = \frac{2\pi}{\beta}$$

In free space: $v_p = c$, $\eta_0 = \sqrt{\mu_0/\varepsilon_0} \approx 377\,\Omega$.

4.3 Lossy Media

For a medium with conductivity $\sigma$: $\gamma = j\omega\sqrt{\mu\varepsilon_c}$ where $\varepsilon_c = \varepsilon - j\sigma/\omega$.

$$\alpha = \omega\sqrt{\frac{\mu\varepsilon}{2}}\left[\sqrt{1+\left(\frac{\sigma}{\omega\varepsilon}\right)^2} - 1\right]^{1/2}$$ $$\beta = \omega\sqrt{\frac{\mu\varepsilon}{2}}\left[\sqrt{1+\left(\frac{\sigma}{\omega\varepsilon}\right)^2} + 1\right]^{1/2}$$ $$\eta = \sqrt{\frac{j\omega\mu}{\sigma + j\omega\varepsilon}}$$

The wave amplitude decays as $e^{-\alpha z}$ — energy is absorbed by the medium.

4.4 Skin Effect

In a good conductor ($\sigma \gg \omega\varepsilon$):

Eq. 4.2 $$\delta_s = \frac{1}{\alpha} = \sqrt{\frac{2}{\omega\mu\sigma}}$$

The skin depth $\delta_s$ is the distance at which field amplitude drops to $1/e$. At high frequencies, currents are confined to a thin surface layer.

For good conductors: $\alpha \approx \beta \approx \sqrt{\pi f\mu\sigma}$, $\eta = (1+j)\frac{1}{\sigma\delta_s}$.

4.5 Polarization

The polarization of a wave describes the trace of the $\mathbf{E}$-field vector tip as a function of time at a fixed point.

$$\mathbf{E} = E_{x0}\cos(\omega t - \beta z)\,\hat{\mathbf{x}} + E_{y0}\cos(\omega t - \beta z + \delta)\,\hat{\mathbf{y}}$$
  • Linear: $\delta = 0$ or $\pi$ — E traces a line
  • Circular: $E_{x0} = E_{y0}$, $\delta = \pm\pi/2$ — E traces a circle (RHCP for $-\pi/2$, LHCP for $+\pi/2$)
  • Elliptical: General case — E traces an ellipse

4.6 Poynting Vector for Plane Waves

The time-average power density:

$$\langle\mathbf{S}\rangle = \frac{1}{2}\text{Re}\{\tilde{\mathbf{E}}\times\tilde{\mathbf{H}}^*\} = \frac{|E_0|^2}{2\eta}\,e^{-2\alpha z}\,\hat{\mathbf{z}}$$

In lossless media ($\alpha = 0$): $\langle S\rangle = \frac{E_0^2}{2\eta}$, constant along $z$.

4.7 Normal Incidence at an Interface

A plane wave hits the boundary between two media ($\eta_1$, $\eta_2$) at normal incidence:

Eq. 4.3 $$\Gamma = \frac{\eta_2 - \eta_1}{\eta_2 + \eta_1}, \qquad \tau = \frac{2\eta_2}{\eta_2 + \eta_1}$$

$\Gamma$ = reflection coefficient, $\tau$ = transmission coefficient. Conservation: $1 + \Gamma = \tau$.

In medium 1, the total field is a superposition of incident and reflected waves:

$$\tilde{E}_1 = E_0(e^{-j\beta_1 z} + \Gamma e^{j\beta_1 z})$$

The standing-wave ratio (SWR):

$$\text{SWR} = \frac{1 + |\Gamma|}{1 - |\Gamma|}$$

SWR = 1 means perfect transmission (no reflection).

4.8 Radiation Pressure

Electromagnetic waves carry momentum. The time-average radiation pressure on a perfect absorber:

$$P = \frac{\langle S\rangle}{c}$$

For a perfect reflector, the pressure doubles: $P = \frac{2\langle S\rangle}{c}$.
